# Philadelphia Civic Engagement Statistics

> Data compiled from official Philadelphia sources and verified public records as of April 2026.

## Philly311 Service Request Data

### Volume
- **Average daily requests:** ~2,400 cases/day
- **Monthly volume:** ~30,000 cases/month
- **Annual volume:** ~360,000+ service requests/year (estimated from monthly figures)
- **Data available since:** December 8, 2014

### SLA Compliance
- **Resolution within SLA timelines:** ~57% of requests (2024)

## Voter Turnout

### 2023 Municipal Election (Mayoral)
- **Turnout:** ~28% of registered voters
- **Context:** Cherelle L. Parker elected as 100th Mayor of Philadelphia

### 2024 Presidential Election
- **Turnout:** ~65% of registered voters
- **Ballots cast:** 726,692
- **Trend:** Decline of ~1.3 percentage points vs. 2020
- **Voter drop:** ~22,600 fewer voters than 2020
- **Ward-level data:** Turnout declined in 56 of 66 political wards

## Public Sentiment (2024-2025)

### "Wrong Track" Perception
- **65%** of respondents believe things in Philadelphia are "pretty seriously" off on the wrong track
- Source: Survey analyzed by WHYY/Philadelphia Inquirer

### #1 Barrier to Employment
- **Transportation** identified as the top barrier to employment in community needs assessments
- Directly relevant to transit, infrastructure, and economic opportunity policy domains

## Mayor Parker's Philly Stat 360

Launched December 2024 as a government performance dashboard to track progress across six priority areas:

| Priority Area | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Safer** | Crime reduction, public safety outcomes, violence intervention |
| **Cleaner** | Street cleaning, litter, illegal dumping, sanitation metrics |
| **Greener** | Parks, tree canopy, sustainability, environmental quality |
| **Economic Opportunity** | Jobs, business growth, workforce development, commercial corridors |
| **Core Services** | 311 response times, infrastructure maintenance, permitting efficiency |
| **Housing & Wellness** | Affordable housing, homelessness reduction, public health outcomes |

**Dashboard URL:** https://philly-stat-360.phila.gov/

## Using This Data

When generating civic intelligence outputs for Philadelphia residents:

1. **Cite specific numbers** -- e.g., "Abandoned vehicles are the #1 complaint category with 8,314 reports in 2024 alone" rather than vague claims
2. **Reference SLA gaps** -- the 57% SLA compliance rate is a powerful data point for advocacy letters arguing for better service delivery
3. **Use voter turnout strategically** -- the 28% municipal turnout vs. 65% presidential turnout highlights civic engagement gaps that strengthen arguments for local action
4. **Connect to Philly Stat 360 categories** -- mapping a citizen's issue to one of the six priority areas shows alignment with the Mayor's stated goals
5. **Link to data sources** -- including OpenDataPhilly or Philly Stat 360 URLs in policy briefs adds credibility
